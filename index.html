Who We Are

MAGIS Service Organization is an all-male Service Organization at Loyola Marymount University that focusses on issues surrounding homelessness and education.


History

Magis is a Latin word for "more" and has been interpreted and used in Ignatian Spirituality as "doing The More" or doing what is better for others. MAGIS Service Organization was established in 2003 under the ambition of striving to do "the more", in congruence with our 3 Pillars: Service, Diversity, and Spirituality.

Our Service Placements

Verbum Dei High School

An all-male Jesuit high school located in Watts, CA, members engage in tutoring and after school activities, ensuring that the student body maintains a 100% acceptance rate to colleges.

Safe Place For Youth

Safe Place for Youth is a shelter for youth experiencing the effects of Homelessness. Throughout the week, members serve at the Los Angeles placement by working directly with the youth.

Good Shepherd Shelter

Good Shepherd Shelter is a shelter for women and their children escaping domestic abuse. Members work with children through helping in the classroom, tutoring, and participating in after school activities.

APPLICATION FAQs


WHEN CAN I APPLY?

All LMU Service Organizations recruit at the beginning of the Spring Semester.


AM I ELIGIBLE?

All applicants must be male freshmen, sophomores, or juniors with a minimum 2.5 GPA.


WHERE DO I APPLY?

All applications can be found on the website of the Center of Service and Action.
